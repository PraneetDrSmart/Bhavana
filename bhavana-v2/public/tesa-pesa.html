Percutaneous Epididymal Sperm Aspiration (PESA) and Testicular Sperm Aspiration (TESA) are two highly effective surgical sperm retrieval methods designed for men with azoospermia, a condition where there is an absence of sperm in the semen.

These procedures help men with obstructive and non-obstructive azoospermia father a child through Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection (ICSI) by retrieving viable sperm directly from the epididymis or testis.

Dr. Bhavana, MBBS, D.G.O (DNB), the Best Fertility Specialist in Suchitra, Kompally, recommends TESA and PESA for men facing fertility challenges such as previous vasectomy, failed vasectomy reversal, seminal tract blockages, congenital absence of the vas deferens, or extremely low sperm count due to medical conditions, infections, or injuries.

These sperm retrieval techniques provide hope for couples struggling with male infertility by offering an alternative way to achieve conception.

PESA (Percutaneous Epididymal Sperm Aspiration) is a minimally invasive procedure used in cases of obstructive azoospermia, where sperm is extracted directly from the epididymis using a fine needle and syringe.

The retrieved sperm is then used in ICSI to fertilize the egg. On the other hand, TESA (Testicular Sperm Aspiration) is performed for men with non-obstructive azoospermia, where sperm is retrieved directly from the testicles through a needle aspiration biopsy (NAB) if no sperm is found in the epididymis.

Both TESA and PESA are quick, outpatient procedures that allow patients to return to normal activities within 4-5 days. Post-procedure care includes wearing scrotal support for 48 hours to reduce discomfort and protect the testicles. While mild pain, tenderness, or slight swelling may occur, these side effects are temporary and can be managed with medications.

To ensure higher success rates, Dr. Bhavana, the Best Fertility Doctor in Suchitra, Kompally incorporates advanced fertility techniques such as IMSI (Intracytoplasmic Morphologically Selected Sperm Injection), calcium activation for improved fertilization, and sperm activation agents to enhance embryo quality. Additionally, cryopreservation options are available for future fertility treatments, allowing patients to preserve sperm for later use.

If you or your partner are experiencing male infertility, TESA and PESA can provide a safe and effective solution to help achieve your parenthood dream.
